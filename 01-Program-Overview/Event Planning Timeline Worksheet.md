# Build with us: Support and Knowledge Base for Beginners — Event Planning Timeline Worksheet

A fill-in worksheet for the **event organizer** to lock in every date leading up to the event. You don't have an event date yet — that's fine. Pencil in a target, work backward, and adjust as it firms up.

Companion documents: [High Level Timeline](High%20Level%20Timeline.md) (what happens in each phase) · [Preparation Checklist](Preparation%20Checklist.md) (what to do) · [Weekly Readiness Meeting Agenda](../04-Facilitator-Guides/Weekly%20Readiness%20Meeting%20Agenda.md).

---

## How to Use This Worksheet

1. **Set the anchor.** Fill in the **Event Day 1 date** below. Everything else is measured backward from it.
2. **Work backward.** Each milestone lists a **recommended timing** as *T‑minus* (weeks/days before Event Day 1). Calculate the calendar date and write it in **Your target date**.
3. **Re-baseline freely.** When the real date is confirmed, update the anchor and recompute. Keep this worksheet as the single source of truth for dates.

---

## Key Assumptions (adjust to fit your program)

- The readiness period is **4 weeks** of weekly meetings (Weeks 1–4). Change the count if your program is shorter or longer, and shift the dates accordingly.
- The **Kickoff** meeting is combined with the **Week 1** readiness meeting.
- The **Final Readiness Review (Go/No-Go)** is **1 week before** the event.
- The event is **2 consecutive days**.
